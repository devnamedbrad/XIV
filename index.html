Final Fantasy XIV

The world of Hydaelyn

The world of Hydaelyn is a vast and varied land. Many races call this star home, with nearly as many gods to match. Hydaelyn, named after the highest god, has had a rich history of civilizations and calamities. With each era of strife and death - the Umbral Eras, came a lasting time of peace - the Astral Eras. It is currently the year 1572 of the 6th Astral Era.

Our story begins in the collection of lands called Eorzea. There are several city-states on this continent that live in relative peace despite minor border disputes, previous wars, or piracy. In 1500, the first Eorzean Alliance was created, but it did not last long.

Ul'dah, shining gem of the deserts of Thanalan. Ruled by Sultana Nanamo Ul Namo, who took the throne at the young age of 5. Despite her now decade in power, the citizens of Ul’dah know that the true power in their city is owned by the Monetarists, the richest members of their land. Among them are people who truly want the best for Ul’dah, and others who will sell their own mother for profit. The people of Ul’dah primarily pray to the twin gods Nald'thal, said to weigh the value of their deeds upon death.

Limsa Lominsa, a thriving and beautiful sea port with vast farmland and expanding port cities along the La Noscean coast. Recently their newest Admiral, Merylwyb, has militarized their fleets and pirates - turning them into privateers who are allowed to only assault the ships of the Garlean Empire. La Noscea is known for incredible metalworks, ale and of course, vicious warriors who fear neither gale nor foe. The people of Limsa aren’t the most religious, but their naval forces will always pray to Llymlaen, goddess of the seas, for a safe voyage.

Gridania, a city that is engulfed in the land itself. The circle of life is present in nearly aspect of their lives. Wood Wailers protect their borders from beast tribes, but also work to keep populations of animals under control. Their leader is selected among an incredibly rare group of Hyur, called the Padjal, who act as mediators between the elementals of the Shroud, and the people of their city. Most citizens pray to Nophica, the Matron, for both blessed harvests and protection from evil.

The Holy See of Ishgard, shining city on the mount, overlooking the dominion of Coerthas. Ruled by Bishop Thordan VII, this land is devoted to the goddess Halone, the Fury. Though once an active member of the old Eorzean Alliance, Ishgard has retreated into its own lands to continue a now 1500 year old war with the Dravanian Horde. Their gates have been all but closed to outsiders, and to some of their own citizens.

Ala Mhigo, a martial nation that was once a force to be reckoned with. Controlling much of the trade between Eorzea and the east, Ala Mhigo quickly gained power. The nation was devoted to Rhalgr, the Destroyer, and had powerful monks and spearmen who worked to be as mighty as him. In 1500 their king sought more land from their neighbor Gridania, and were subdued by the first Eorzean Alliance. In 1557, in the midst of a civil war, Ala Mhigo fell to the Garlean Empire. Its people either displaced or made to submit, the now occupied city-state remains a husk of what it once was. 

Garlemald is, for the most part, a mystery. Though the land was once mapped and had smaller neighboring states, the Empire discovered a fuel they dubbed “ceruleum” and quickly made warmachines their neighbors could not defeat. The Empire has taken over their own island continent of Ilsabard, the eastern continent of Othard, and now part of Eorzea has been brought to heel. It is unknown how their emperor, Solus zos Galvus, united his nation, but under his rule it has grown immensely. 

After its success in Ala Mhigo, in 1562 the Garlean Empire began encroaching on the western coast of Eorzea to pinch the nations and breach the font of aetheric magic they believed Silvertear Lake to be. Bringing its largest airship, the Agrius, the Garleans hoped to overwhelm the forces of Eorzea. Their plan however was foiled by the awakening of the largest dragon ever seen. It encircled the ship and brought it into Silvertear lake. The ship exploded and a massive surge of aether from within the lake was released into the world. From it came a swelling of power, and rumors of the beast tribes summoning “primals” of eld. The corpses of the Agrius and the great wyrm can be seen to this day, though all avoid the “keeper of the lake”.

10 years later we find you, adventurer, starting your life amidst the chaos of what a few people call the start of the Seventh Umbral Era.
